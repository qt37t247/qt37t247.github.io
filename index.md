I'm a researcher working at the intersection of **ecology**, **evolution**, and **genetics**.
My work uses genomic data and spatially explicit simulations to understand how species
respond to environmental change — from urbanisation and habitat loss to the accidental
dispersal of invasive insects across oceans.

## Research interests

Much of my research sits in three overlapping areas:

- **Landscape and population genomics** — how the physical structure of a landscape
  (roads, rivers, urban fabric) shapes gene flow in birds, mosquitoes, horseshoe crabs,
  and cockroaches.
- **Invasion biology and global dispersal** — reconstructing the evolutionary history of
  widely distributed species, including the 250‑year mystery of the German cockroach's
  global spread.
- **Conservation genomics under habitat loss** — building simulation tools (e.g.
  [ResDisMapper](https://github.com/qt37t247/ResDisMapper) and spatially explicit
  forward‑time models) to forecast genetic diversity loss under realistic
  non‑equilibrium scenarios.
